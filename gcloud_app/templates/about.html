About the Project

Air travel is expected to boom post-covid, but what constitutes a great flight experience?

We built a model to predict whether a passenger would rank their experience as satisfactory or not depending on their survey results.

Using this model and our analysis, an airline could identify which flight features to focus on to ensure customer satisfaction and, in turn, profitability.

Random Forest model score >96% accuracy. See for yourself.

Using a dataset from Kaggle, we reviewed ~140,000 flyer surveys spanning 17 aspects of air travel and their ranking of importance to the traveler.

Through analysis we determined Random Forest to be the best model fit for our data. You can see machine learning metrics for our model on the Metric tab in the navigation bar. You can use the model by completing a survey on the Homepage.

To complete this project we utilized the following:

  • Python Pandas
  • Scikit-Learn
  • HTML/CSS/Bootstrap
  • Amazon AWS
  • Heroku

Recommendations and next steps.

1. Review % of surveys completed vs passengers flying. Ensure getting a good mix of types of flyers and across reasonable timeframe

2. Update survey to be more concise and less subjectivity. Current survey has 1-5 ranking leaving room for passenger to be iffy; suggest a yes/no approach Focus on top 3 satisfiers and bottom 3 dissatisfiers; 17 factors is too many

3. Cluster passengers based on demographics/flying behaviors to understand their preferences; incorporate their spending habits to determine if focusing on their satisifiers will increase profitabilty
